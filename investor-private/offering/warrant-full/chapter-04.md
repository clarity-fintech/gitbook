Communications, Disclosures, and Investor FAQ The regulatory memo describes a “structural communications doctrine” that discourages price predictions, promotional yield statements, or marketing that could worsen securities-risk analysis.

That doctrine should be formalized in the definitive Warrant package: all external communications should focus on product utility, network architecture, rights, restrictions, and risk disclosure rather than speculative return language.

[Source](https://www.genspark.ai/api/files/s/DvuzNLhF) Draft FAQ Question Draft institutional answer What assets can I use?

Proposed accepted contribution assets include BTC, ETH, SOL, and selected stablecoins, subject to treasury support, compliance screening, and final subscription instructions.

How is my allocation calculated?

Based on the USD-equivalent value of the accepted contribution at the relevant settlement timestamp, adjusted by the active sale or allocation tier and any applicable warrant terms.

When do I receive CLRTY?

At launch or a defined claim event, subject to compliance clearance, technical readiness, vesting or escrow conditions, and definitive agreement terms.

Is there a cap?

The source materials consistently reference a 16,000,000 CLRTY hard cap; definitive issuance evidence should confirm the live genesis and any mirrored supply architecture.

Who is this for?

Strategic capital partners, ecosystem builders, developers, traders, liquidity providers, and other eligible participants who can satisfy the legal and compliance requirements of the applicable offering channel.
