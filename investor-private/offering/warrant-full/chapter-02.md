CLRTY Ecosystem and Technical Architecture The yellowpaper describes clrty-1 as a sovereign layer-1 deterministic state machine with a native asset called uclrty.

It presents a formal state-transition model, validator logic, state commitments, BFT-style finality requirements, and multiple node classes, including public, private, strategy, compliance, and DePIN-oriented roles.

The same paper positions PRISM as an intelligence plane and HELIX as an execution and routing plane, with deterministic post-state convergence remaining normative regardless of higher-layer routing logic.

[Source](https://www.genspark.ai/api/files/s/FXsMSI9M) The investor whitepaper and computational substrate paper extend that architecture into a more commercially framed design language.

These materials describe CLRTY as a “computational commodity,” a token with behavior-aware logic, and an asset embedded in a broader software stack rather than a stand-alone speculative coin.

They also introduce a 99-to-1 set system or Continuous Classification & Rebalancing model under which wallet behavior may affect ranking, priority, fee shielding, or access conditions.

Those concepts are source-supported as project design features, but their live production scope should be presented as model-driven and subject to implementation proof.

[Source](https://www.genspark.ai/api/files/s/o7YESSGt) [Source](https://www.genspark.ai/api/files/s/EcNBb5ta) Architecture layers referenced clrty-1 settlement layer; PRISM intelligence and audit layer; HELIX execution/routing layer; wallet/account abstractions; developer SDKs; node deployment tooling; research and simulation artifacts.

Cautionary framing The yellowpaper itself distinguishes normative definitions from implementation notes, modeled results, and theory framing.

Some concepts are explicitly treated as aspirational or not fully evidenced in public production environments.

The public GitHub repositories partially reinforce the ecosystem thesis.

The PRISM CLI repository advertises a chain-native CLI spanning PRISM, HELIX, and “skills”; the developer kit describes SDKs, node-control APIs, wallet builder frameworks, testing suites, and deployment tooling; the node deployment kit outlines public, private, strategy, compliance, and DePIN node categories; and the wallet integration repository presents a partner kit, manifests, SDK packages, and portal components.

Those repositories do not prove live commercial scale, but they do support the claim that developer, operator, and integration artifacts are being organized publicly.

Token Supply, Integrity, and Utility Framework Across the source set, the most consistent economic statement is the fixed supply position: CLRTY is repeatedly described as hard capped at 16,000,000 tokens, typically with 9 decimals.

Several materials go further and state that mint authority should be nullified or disabled at genesis and that post-genesis minting is not intended to exist.

In a warrant context, that scarcity narrative matters because it informs dilution expectations, treasury discipline, and investor reliance on supply integrity rather than discretionary issuance.

CLRTY is described in different source classes as execution fuel, gas-like metering, governance weight, validator or node-bonding support, staking-linked participation asset, service-billing rail, settlement primitive, developer access enabler, and token-aware software licensing medium.

The investor whitepaper further emphasizes B2C, B2B, and B2B2B value routes, while the yellowpaper formalizes native-asset semantics within the clrty-1 state machine.

In definitive legal drafting, these uses should be listed as stated or intended utility domains, not guaranteed real-world adoption metrics.

Contribution Mechanics and Accepted Assets The user-supplied commercial language states that accepted contribution assets include BTC, ETH, SOL, and selected stablecoins; that allocation is based on USD value at contribution time and the active sale tier; and that a full receipt is provided with transaction details.

This language is commercially coherent and aligns with the idea of a deterministic allocation model, but the uploaded primary documents do not uniformly specify the full accepted-asset list in final legal terms.

Accordingly, this draft treats the list as proposed offering language pending confirmation in definitive subscription materials.

The term-sheet and financing materials are more specific on process than on consumer-style checkout language.

They describe capital commitments via wire or stablecoins, protocol-native or atomic settlement flows, KYC/AML portal steps, ledger-anchored confirmations, and vesting-schedule assignment.

A final warrant should therefore reconcile the high-conversion website copy with the institutionally gated mechanics described in the financing documents.

Three-Stage Allocation System The user supplied a three-stage commercial allocation framework: Stage 1 “Genesis Access,” Stage 2 “Builder Window,” and Stage 3 “Public Activation.” This framework is not contradicted by the broader source set, though the core whitepaper and yellowpaper materials do not themselves formalize it as the exclusive sale taxonomy.

Instead, the source papers discuss private seed, tranches, staged liquidity seeding, exchange pathways, and post-launch ecosystem access.

This draft therefore integrates the three-stage system as a commercial overlay that must be reconciled with definitive tranche and vesting documentation.

Eligibility, Suitability, and Compliance Gating The private placement memorandum is clear that the referenced financing is restricted to accredited investors under Rule 501 of Regulation D, with investors expected to certify sophistication, risk tolerance, and investment intent.

The term sheet adds authority, sanctions, and lawful-funds representations.

Even if portions of the broader CLRTY ecosystem ultimately target developers, traders, and liquidity providers more broadly, this Warrant should preserve the stricter eligibility standard where the instrument functions as a private financing right rather than a public consumer purchase flow.
