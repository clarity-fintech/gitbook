X.

Subscription and Payment Process The Company’s current intended payment process is designed to combine institutional compliance with speed and reduced friction.

The following draft process is proposed as the operative subscription workflow for the current raise.

A.

Qualification and Approval The prospective investor initiates interest through the Company’s approved channel, which may include a Company-hosted portal, institutional contact workflow, secure dashboard, or direct coordination with the Company.

The investor then completes identity verification, accreditation review, sanctions screening, and source-of-funds checks.

Where available, wallet-linked or enterprise identity verification may be used to streamline the process, but no investor shall be deemed accepted until the Company has completed its compliance review.

This reflects both your supplied “direct-entry” model and the compliance-gated architecture described in Company materials.

Regulatory Opinion Memo B.

Execution of Subscription Documents Once approved, the investor receives or accesses the applicable subscription materials, which may include a SAFE, SAFT, subscription agreement, investor questionnaire, risk disclosures, and, where appropriate, a side letter or rights schedule.

The Company may permit digital execution through a secure portal, approved e-signature method, or contract-integrated signature flow.

Any “one-click” or “atomic” execution language should be understood as a target operational design in which legal acceptance, compliance status, and settlement are coordinated in one streamlined flow, rather than a waiver of documentary requirements.

The Company’s materials support strong interest in smart-contract-assisted execution, but final enforceability remains subject to counsel-approved implementation.

Regulatory Opinion Memo C.

Settlement Methods Following approval and execution readiness, the investor shall receive settlement instructions through an approved channel.

Settlement may occur in either: (1) USD via wire transfer to a designated account or escrow arrangement identified by the Company; or (2) approved stable-asset equivalents, such as USDC or USDT, transmitted to a Company-designated and compliance-approved receiving address, vault, or escrow wallet.

The Company’s public planning page expressly references capital raised into a multisig treasury in USDC, USDT, and ETH and mapped to vested allocations.

This supports use of stable-asset settlement as part of the round, provided all applicable compliance, custody, and documentation conditions are met.

XI.

Streamlined Onboarding Model for Institutional Participants The Company may describe its onboarding architecture in the application as an Automated Infrastructure-Led Onboarding or One-Click Institutional Subscription model.

In plain terms, that means the Company aims to minimize administrative friction by consolidating identity verification, agreement execution, and payment initiation into a unified workflow.

A prospective institutional investor may connect a compliant wallet, treasury account, or corporate identity profile; complete real-time or near-real-time accreditation and KYC/AML review; execute the required subscription instrument digitally; and settle the commitment through either fiat or approved stable assets.

The objective is to reduce manual back-and-forth while maintaining multi-signature treasury security and traceable confirmation.

This framing is commercially strong and consistent with your supplied process language, but final deployment of fully atomic, single-transaction execution should be described as a planned or supported pathway rather than an unconditional present-state guarantee unless independently verified in counsel-reviewed final docs.

Suggested concise application framing: “The Company has designed an automated institutional subscription workflow intended to combine compliance verification, digital execution, and secure settlement into a streamlined onboarding process.
